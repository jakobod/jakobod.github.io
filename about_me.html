So... Who am I?

I am Jakob, a C++ software engineer working as a runtime framework developer in the automotive industry. During my bachelors degree, I was able to try out many things ranging from developing autonomous cars to working on frameworks for distributed systems. In my second year, I started as a research assistant in the INET group at the University of Applied Sciences in Hamburg where I had the opportunity to become part of the C++ Actor Framework (CAF) development team. Learning to write modern and efficient code in C++ lead me to my special interest in distributed and time-critical systems. I ended up writing my bachelors thesis about the design and implementation of the network stack in CAF and also published a paper about it at the Agere 2020. After getting my bachelors degree, I started the studies for my masters degree in computer sciences with focus on distributed and time-critical systems. Simultaneous to my progress in education, I started a job as C++ software engineer developing a runtime framework for LIDAR based environment perception algorithms.

In my free time I try to constantly challenge myself with different projects, such as 3D printing, server administration, or embedded programming. This website was one of these projects as well, where I wanted to learn about writing websites. When I am not working on computer related things, I like to go climbing, biking, or sailing. Quite recently I finished my motorcycle license so you might meet me cruising through the streets on my bike.

I am always open for new and interesting challenges in the field of C++ development - so please, get in touch with me if you have an offer.
